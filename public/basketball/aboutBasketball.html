What to know about Basketball:
About the sport:

Basketball is the only major sport strictly of U.S. orgin and it was created by James Naismith in 1891. It started off as two peach baskets as on a pole on each side of the court and a ball made from panels of leather stitched together with a rubber bladder inside and a cloth lining for support. Fun the fact the first game (not really but the test game) only one point was scored and it was a midcourt shot.

Rules of the game:

There are alot of rules to the game but there are the main five that everyone follows. Must be played with a light ball(Ex - a basketball); Must be handled with the hands; No running with the ball; no player being restricted from getting the ball when it is in play; No personal contact: and you must score on a horizontal elevated goal.
How to move with the ball: A player may pass or bounce (or driblle the ball) to their teamate so they can potentially try and get a score.
Fouling: A foul is committed whenever a player makes such contact with an opponent as to put him at a disadvantage. if a player is fouled while trying to shoot and the shot is good, the point is rewarded. If a foul is committed against a player who is not shooting, then his team is awarded either the possession of the ball or a free throw if the other team is in a penalty situation.Infractions such as unsportsmanlike conduct or grasping the rim are technical fouls, which award to the opposition a free throw and possession of the ball. Overly violent fouls are called flagrant fouls and also result in free throws and possession for the opposition. Players are allowed a set number of personal fouls per game (six in the NBA, five in most other competitions) and are removed from the game when the foul limit is reached.
Penalties: When the team is awarded a penalty they are allowed to score some points on the free throw line. If the basket was made when the player was fouled, then the basket counts and one free throw is awarded. If the basket was not made, then either two free throws or three are awarded.

Scoring Points:

For 1 Point: You can get 1 point for each free throw you get and these are usually awrded when the team gets a penalty.
For 2 Points: You can shoot anywhere in the paint or inside the half-circle. This where most points are scored during games and is also one of the challenging scoring areas of basketball in general.
For 3 Points: These are only scored from the three point line and beyond. These types of baskets get the most hype of any crowd since it is one of the toughest shots to make espacially when your in motion. Sometimes games are decided when there is little time remaining and one player decides to take a hal court shot to try to win the game.
